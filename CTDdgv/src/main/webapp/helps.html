1. What Information and Functions are provided in the CTDdgv?

Circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) variants hold significant promise as cancer biomarkers in liquid biopsies, due to their minimally invasive nature and ability to capture the comprehensive tumor landscape. Identifying and interpreting driver genes and variants in ctDNA from body fluids is crucial for developing personalized treatment strategies, predicting disease prognosis, and assessing therapeutic efficacy.

Here, we present CTDdgv (http://bio-bigdata.hrbmu.edu.cn/CTDdgv), a web server that provides researchers with an advanced platform for identifying TDGs and TDVs in ctDNA. Additionally, CTDdgv enables users to access clinical and functional information for TDGs and TDVs of interest.



2. What data are Included in the CTDdgv?

CTDdgv manually curates data from 4,797 published studies as of May 8, 2024. Variants with clinically relevant interpretations are categorized into two main groups. The Variant-Level group comprises 82 diseases, 158 genes, 12 mutation types, and 865 key missense mutations, while the Gene-Level group includes 58 diseases and 132 genes. Additionally, the Prediction-Level data includes 2,305 tumor driver genes and 23,012 tumor driver variants identified from 38 publicly available ctDNA mutation profiles, encompassing 21 cancer types.

3.4.1 geMERlb

Design of geMERlb pipeline for identifying TDGs and TDVs in ctDNA

We introduce geMERlb (Genomic Element Mutation Enrichment Research in Liquid Biopsy) to identify tumor driver genes (TDGs) and variants (TDVs) in ctDNA by integrating nonsynonymous somatic mutations of liquid biopsy and the sequence information of genomic elements. The genomic element set containing the coding sequences (CDS) (n = 20,185), splice sites (n = 18,729), 3' untranslated regions (3' UTRs) (n = 19,369), 5' untranslated regions (5' UTRs) (n =19,188), and promoters (n = 20,164) were generated by PCAWG and downloaded from DriverPower. Their genome coordinates were converted from GENCODE v37 to GENCODE v38 by Liftover to align with somatic mutations annotated in different reference genomes.

3.4.2 Upload files

In the tools module, users can upload ctDNA mutation spectra (required). It will calculate tumor driver genes and variants through the geMERlb pipeline. If users also upload the patient's survival data (optional), the server could evaluate the prognostic significance of driver genes.

Variant.txt (Fig 1) and Survival.txt (Fig 2) file format instruction:

1. The 'Tumor_Sample_Barcode' column as patient ID.

2. The 'Chromosome' column as the chromosome where the mutation is located.

3. The 'Start_Position' column as the starting position of the mutation on the genome.

4. The 'End_Position' column as the ending position of the mutation on the genome.

5. The 'Hugo_Symbol' column as gene symbol.

6. The 'Reference' column as the nucleotide sequence before the mutation, using '_' to indicate unknown bases.

7. The 'Alteration' column as the nucleotide sequence after the mutation, using '_' to indicate unknown bases.

8. The 'Mutation_type' column as the mutation type, using '_' to indicate unknown types. allowed types include:

'MISSENSE', 'EXON', 'FRAMESHIFT', 'NONSENSE', 'SILENT', 'PROTEINDEL', 'PROTEININS', 'SPLICE_REGION', 'SPLICE', 'INTRON', 'UTR_3', 'UTR_5', 'NONSTANDARD', 'NONCODING', 'SNV', 'MNV', 'insertion', 'Small_deletion'.

9. The 'patient_id' column as patient ID.

10. The 'status' column as the survival or disease progression status of the patient at the last follow-up.

11. The 'PFS' as progression-free survival, which can be replaced by 'OS' (overall survival).

3.7 Submit

The "Submit" page provides a strategy for sharing experiments validated variants data for CTDdgv update. The submitter is required to fill in the relevant information for the variant, as well as the user's e-mail. Please use "-" instead of in the unknown option. Refer to the following example:

1. "PaperDoi" should be filled with the DOI number of the reference literature.

2. "DiseaseName" should be filled with the name of the disease related to the experimentally verified variant.

3. "Gene" should be filled with the gene symbol involved in the experimentally validated variant, such as TP53, KRAS, GNA11, etc.

4. "VariantType" should be filled with the type of the experimentally validated variant, such as Missense, methylation, indel, etc.

5. "VariantDescription" should be filled with a detailed description of the experimentally validated mutation, such as G12C, c.1789A>T, PAX3-NCOA1 fusion, etc.

6. "BodyFluid" should be filled with the type of body fluid used in the liquid biopsy, including plasma, urine, cerebrospinal fluid, pleural effusion, sputum, semen, etc.

7. "Experiment" should be filled with the experimental method or a summary of the experimental process.

8. "Function" should be filled with the clinical function (interpretation) related to the experimentally validated variant.

9. "SequenceMethod" should be filled with the sequencing technique used in the liquid biopsy, such as NGS, ddPCR, etc.

10. "Email" should be filled with the email address of the submitter.
